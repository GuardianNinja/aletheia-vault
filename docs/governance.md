# Governance

This document describes the governance model for Aletheia Vault as operated
by Space Leaf Corp (Non‑Profit), including:

- Roles and responsibilities,
- Decision-making processes,
- Oversight of the JARVONDIS Reserve,
- Enforcement of the Space Leaf Corp Non‑Profit License (SLC‑NPL 1.0),
- Community participation and transparency.

The governance model is designed to keep power shared, accountable, and
aligned with the mission of stewardship and sustainability.

---

## 1. Core principles

1. **Stewardship over ownership**
   - No single person or private entity “owns” the system.
   - Space Leaf Corp acts as a steward on behalf of the community.

2. **Transparency**
   - Decisions, policies, and key metrics should be visible and understandable.

3. **Community benefit**
   - Policies should prioritize creators, learners, and future generations.

4. **Accountability**
   - Clear roles, logs, and checks so that decisions can be reviewed and corrected.

5. **Non‑profit integrity**
   - All uses should uphold the SLC‑NPL 1.0 mission alignment conditions.

---

## 2. Roles

### 2.1 Inventors & Contributors

- Submit inventions and improvements.
- Choose license preferences.
- Participate in governance discussions and feedback channels.
- Can propose:
  - Policy refinements,
  - New license templates,
  - New reserve uses aligned with mission.

### 2.2 Licensees (Organizations, Labs, Companies)

- Use the platform to license inventions.
- Obligated to:
  - Report usage honestly,
  - Follow license terms,
  - Respect transparency requirements.
- May participate in advisory roles but do not control core governance.

### 2.3 Investors

- Provide funding via the Investor Market.
- Receive revenue-share payouts per contracts.
- May participate in advisory and signal-based governance (non-binding),
  but cannot override community or non-profit objectives.

### 2.4 Franchise Operators

- Operate regional or sector-specific deployments under franchise licenses.
- Must:
  - Adhere to platform standards and quality requirements,
  - Respect 2% weekly contribution logic where applicable,
  - Implement transparency dashboards.

### 2.5 Moderators / Stewards

- Handle:
  - Content moderation (spam, abuse, obvious violations).
  - Simple dispute resolution (e.g., duplicate inventions).
- Act under clear written policies.

### 2.6 Governance Council

The central decision-making body for:

- Policy and license updates.
- Reserve usage rules and exceptional withdrawals.
- System-level disputes and appeals.
- Approving major architectural or economic changes.

**Composition:**

- A mix of:
  - Inventors/creators.
  - Technical/architectural experts.
  - Legal/IP advisors.
  - Community advocates.
  - Space Leaf Corp representatives.

**Selection:**

- Initial council may be appointed by Space Leaf Corp.
- Over time, some seats may be:
  - Rotated,
  - Elected by verified community members,
  - Reserved for external independent members.

### 2.7 Space Leaf Corp (Non‑Profit Entity)

- Custodian of:
  - Brand and mission.
  - Legal and regulatory interfaces.
  - Core repositories and root DNS / contracts.
- Ensures alignment with:
  - SLC‑NPL 1.0 license.
  - JARVONDIS Reserve policies.
  - Non‑profit statutes.

## 3. Decision-making processes

### 3.1 Policy Changes

Examples:

- Changing default license templates.
- Updating transparency reporting standards.
- Adjusting reserve safety thresholds.

**Process:**

1. Draft proposal (by council member, steward, or community contributor).
2. Public posting and comment period.
3. Governance Council discussion and refinement.
4. Recorded vote of the council.
5. Publication of final text and reasoning.

### 3.2 Technical Changes (Core System)

Examples:

- Major architectural shifts.
- Protocol changes affecting payouts or 2% calculations.
- Smart contract upgrades.

**Process:**

1. Technical RFC (Request For Comments) created in the repo.
2. Discussion with maintainers and community.
3. Security and impact review.
4. Governance Council approval (for changes with economic or legal impact).
5. Staged deployment with rollback plans.

### 3.3 Disputes

Types:

- Inventorship disputes.
- License or payment disputes.
- Misuse of the platform or data.

**Process:**

1. Initial report submitted (by affected party).
2. Triage by Moderators:
   - If simple/clear: resolved at moderator level.
   - If complex: escalated to Governance Council or external neutral mediator.
3. Investigation:
   - Review of logs, contracts, and communications.
4. Ruling:
   - Council issues a decision and recommended remedies.
5. Appeal:
   - Limited appeals may be allowed, per written policy.

## 4. JARVONDIS Reserve governance

The JARVONDIS Reserve is a critical stabilization tool and must be governed carefully.

### 4.1 Inflows

- Governed by:
  - The 2% weekly contribution rule from:
    - Investor Market,
    - Franchise Market,
    - Public Crypto Market.
- The Governance Council may NOT:
  - Reduce the contribution rate below 2% without:
    - Clear justification,
    - Public consultation,
    - Supermajority vote.

### 4.2 Outflows (Use of Reserve Funds)

**Priority order:**

1. Stabilize creator payouts during downturns.
2. Maintain critical platform operations and security.
3. Fund mission-aligned grants or educational programs (only if above safety thresholds).

**Approval:**

- Reserve withdrawals must:
  - Be proposed with:
    - Purpose,
    - Amount,
    - Impact analysis.
  - Be approved by:
    - A specified majority of the Governance Council,
    - Space Leaf Corp representatives,
    - Automated policy checks that enforce safety floors.

### 4.3 Transparency

- Publicly available reserve dashboard:
  - Current balance.
  - Historical contributions (by source).
  - Historical withdrawals (by category).
- Annual or periodic public report:
  - Explaining major reserve decisions.
  - Reviewing performance against goals.

## 5. Enforcement of SLC‑NPL 1.0 License

Governance includes ensuring that deployments and forks respect the license.

### 5.1 Monitoring

- Public deployments of the platform:
  - Encouraged to register with Space Leaf Corp.
- Community members may report:
  - Violations of transparency requirements.
  - Attempts to bypass 2% contributions.
  - Misrepresentations of affiliation.

### 5.2 Response to Violations

- Space Leaf Corp and the Governance Council may:
  - Request corrections.
  - Publicly document non-compliance.
  - Revoke rights under the license (per license terms).
  - Where necessary, pursue legal remedies consistent with non-profit resources
    and mission.

### 5.3 Reinstatement

- Violating parties may:
  - Correct issues,
  - Repay missing contributions,
  - Restore transparency.
- Governance Council may:
  - Choose to reinstate rights under the license,
  - Document the reinstatement and conditions.

---

## 6. Community participation

### 6.1 Feedback Channels

- Public issue trackers.
- Community forums or chat channels.
- Periodic open calls or assemblies.

### 6.2 Advisory Groups

- Topic-focused groups (e.g., security, education, open science).
- Provide recommendations to the Governance Council.

### 6.3 Documentation and Education

- Governance decisions and processes should:
  - Be documented in plain language.
  - Be accessible to non-experts.
- Educational resources:
  - Explain:
    - Licensing model,
    - Payouts model,
    - Reserve purpose,
    - Governance rights and responsibilities.

---

The governance model is intended to evolve over time, but always under the
core principles of stewardship, transparency, and community benefit that
define Space Leaf Corp and the Aletheia Vault ecosystem.
